Art Attelier

Client work

Art Attelier is a ceramics studio, gallery, and event space nestled in the Hudson Valley in New Paltz, NY — founded by Mariu, a fine artist who spent years dreaming of a place where pottery and community could share the same room. I built their visual identity and web presence from scratch: logo, color system, typography, and the Squarespace site that launched their classes, gallery, and event rental programs.

The brand had to do something specific: feel warm and handmade without looking hobbyist. Art Attelier is a serious creative institution — they run juried gallery exhibitions alongside beginner drop-in classes — and the identity needed to hold both of those things at once. Everything below was built to match the physical space: light and earthy, rooted in the Hudson Valley, confident without being precious.

The Mark

A wordmark that reads fine-art-adjacent — not a craft fair, not a gallery on Bleecker. The logotype is set in a warm serif that echoes the organic curve of thrown pottery, paired with clean spacing that signals organization and intentionality.

Used across the website, signage, class materials, and social — consistent enough to build recognition fast with a brand still in its first year.

Classes & Workshops

The site does three jobs simultaneously: sign up new students, showcase the gallery to collectors and artists, and book the event space to private clients. Three completely different audiences, one URL. The architecture keeps each path clean — the homepage communicates warmth and accessibility first, then lets people self-select into whatever they came for.

For Students

Group classes, one-time workshops, RAKU events, life drawing — all with clear scheduling and registration built in.

For Collectors

A dedicated gallery section promoting established and emerging ceramic artists — organized like a real exhibition space, not a shop.

For Event Clients

Indoor gallery and outdoor sculpture garden rental — positioned for private gatherings, weddings, and corporate events in the Hudson Valley.

Squarespace E-commerce Event Booking

The Instagram for a space like this is as much a recruitment tool as it is a portfolio — potential students need to see the environment before they'll show up. Content strategy focused on three things: showing the space and atmosphere (not just finished work), celebrating students alongside featured artists so beginners feel welcome, and creating regular content anchors around class registration windows and upcoming events.

The brand voice is warm and community-first, never precious. The goal is to make ceramics feel accessible to someone who's never touched clay — and to make artists feel like there's a serious, respected platform for their work in the same breath.

BFF

Brooklyn Film Festival

Client work

Programming assistant and on-stage MC for the narrative feature program at one of the largest competitive film festivals in the US — coordinating VIP talent, running screening logistics, and moderating live post-film Q&As.

Film festivals look effortless from the audience. They aren't.

My role at BFF centered on the narrative feature program — the part of the festival where independent directors show up from across the country and need someone to make them feel like the room is ready for them before they walk in front of an audience that just spent 90 minutes with their film.

That meant being the first face a filmmaker saw when they arrived and the last voice the audience heard before they left. VIP talent coordination and logistics before the lights went down. On-stage moderation and Q&A hosting after they came back up. Every screening was a different film, a different director, a different energy in the room — the job was to read all of it and keep the conversation alive.

A live, unscripted Q&A after a feature film is its own discipline. There's no second take if a question goes sideways or the conversation stalls. The skills it builds — reading a room, briefing talent quickly, keeping energy high — are the same ones that show up in every content and brand role I've held since. You just don't always see them on a résumé.

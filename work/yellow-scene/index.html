Yellow Scene Magazine

Client work

I wrote editorial features and ran on-page SEO for Yellow Scene Magazine, a Colorado culture publication. Below are a few of the pieces I wrote — expand any of them for a quick summary and a link to the full article.

A revisit of the Falborn brothers a decade after their original "SuperKids" photo shoot on a west Longmont farm, tracking how four siblings who once spent their days chasing frogs and raising sheep grew into very different adult lives and callings. The piece pairs new interviews with a recreated photo shoot from the original photographer.

Read the full article →

A look at how Erie, Colorado transformed from a small farming town into one of the fastest-growing towns on the Front Range, as families priced out of Boulder and Denver moved in and developers built out mile after mile of new housing. The piece traces the tradeoffs of that growth over the past two and a half decades.

Read the full article →

A dispatch from the Lafayette Peach Festival, where six blocks of downtown filled with vendors, live music, and fair food for one of the biggest local festivals of the year. The piece captures the scale and feel of the event, from the peach vendors to the sprawl of craft and food booths.
